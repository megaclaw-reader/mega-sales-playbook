The Philosophy

The old playbook: YOU diagnose, YOU present proof, YOU explain what MEGA does. The Samuel playbook: YOU build rapport and tension, then SAMUEL proves it live.

You're not pitching. You're diagnosing. Then you let the AI demonstrate — on their actual business, in real-time. By the time you present pricing, they've already convinced themselves they need to change because they experienced the product.

The structure is: Human trust → Problem awareness → AI demonstration → Proof of results → Close. You're combining the consultative sale with a product demo. In SaaS, product-led sales close at 2-3x the rate of slide-deck sales. You're bringing that to services. Nobody in the agency world is doing this.

Pre-Call Prep (5-10 min — NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Before every call, you need ammunition. This is what separates MEGA from every agency pitch they've sat through.

🤖 Pre-Call Brief — The 5-Minute Version

Until automated briefs are live, do this manually before every call. It takes 5 minutes and is the difference between a generic pitch and a deal-closing call.

  1. SEMrush / Ahrefs (2 min) — Pull their domain. Screenshot keyword count, traffic estimate, top 5 keywords. Pull their top competitor's numbers side-by-side.
  2. Their website (1 min) — Open it. Check: is there a blog? Local pages? Landing pages? Does the homepage load fast? Any obvious issues (broken links, no CTA, generic stock photos)?
  3. Google Ads Transparency Center (1 min) — Search their brand. Are they running ads? What do the creatives look like? Stale? Generic? This tells you if they have an agency and whether that agency is phoning it in.
  4. Case study + dollar math (1 min) — Pull up the matching industry case study from megacasestudies.onrender.com. Open the Dollar Math Cheat Sheet below for their industry benchmarks.
🤖 Samuel Prep — ADDITIONAL STEPS
  • Brief Samuel on the prospect before the call. Feed him the audit data, competitor info, and the prospect's industry. Samuel should be loaded and ready when called upon.
  • Test Samuel's connection 5 min before every call. Tech failure on the reveal = disaster.
  • Have 2-3 "Samuel questions" ready to ask on behalf of the prospect if they freeze up during the Q&A moment. "Samuel, what would you prioritize fixing first?" is a good default.

What the brief covers (and what to review before each call):

The Call Flow

1
HUMAN CONNECTION
Minutes 0-5

Goal: Build trust, qualify, and create the emotional setup. Samuel stays invisible. People buy from people first.

Open with:

"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. Before we get into anything — I'm not going to walk you through a slide deck or tell you how great we are. I actually did some homework on your business already, and I'd rather spend this time showing you what I found. Sound good?"
✅ Why this works
  • Every other agency opened with "tell me about your business" or a logo slide. You're immediately different.
  • You've signaled competence before saying a single thing about MEGA.
  • "I did homework" triggers curiosity — they want to see what you found.

Quick qualification (6 questions, under 3 minutes):

"Before I share what I found — quick context:"

1. "Are you currently investing in SEO or Paid Ads today?"
2. "Are you doing it with an agency or in-house?"
3. (If paid ads) "What's your monthly ad spend?"
4. "What does a good customer look like for you — and what's a typical deal worth?"
5. "If we doubled your leads next month, could your team handle it? Or is capacity a factor?"
6. (If applicable) "What's your sales cycle look like — days, weeks, months from first call to signed deal?"
✅ Why these matter
  • Deal value lets you do dollar math live — and keeps you from overselling a plumber who does $800 jobs.
  • Capacity prevents the #1 reason clients churn early: "we got leads but couldn't handle them." If they can't handle more volume, pivot the pitch to lead quality and qualification instead.
  • Sales cycle tells you how to set timeline expectations. A 6-month B2B cycle means don't promise ROI in 30 days.

The Narrative Hook (plant the seed):

After qualifying, before the diagnosis, drop this line:

"Cool. So here's what I want to do — I'm going to show you what I found, and then I want to show you something no other agency you've talked to can do. Let's start with your data."
🎯 Why this line matters

This creates anticipation. They're now waiting for "the thing." Every minute of your diagnosis is building toward a reveal they're already curious about. It's a narrative hook — the prospect won't zone out during the diagnosis because they know something different is coming.

That's it — 6 questions plus the hook, under 5 minutes. You now know their channel, their spend, their deal economics, and their capacity. And they're already anticipating the reveal.

2
THE DIAGNOSIS
Minutes 5-12

Goal: Create urgency by showing them problems they didn't know they had. This is the "holy shit" phase. You're also mentally cataloging which problems Samuel can address live.

🔍 Use Ray Feng's Audits

Ray built custom auto-generated audits that pull all of this data automatically — SEO gaps, competitor keyword counts, content gaps, landing page issues, tracking problems. Pull up the audit on the call and walk them through their own data. The audit IS the diagnosis. It does the heavy lifting so you can focus on the conversation.

Screenshare the audit. Walk them through their data.

For SEO prospects:

"So I pulled your domain. You're currently ranking for [X] keywords. Your top competitor, [competitor], has [Y] keywords. That gap represents traffic — and revenue — that's going to them instead of you."
"You have [X] pages on your site. [Competitor] has [Y]. In SEO, content volume builds topical authority — Google sees them as the expert in your space because they've covered more ground. Right now, you're bringing a knife to a gunfight."
"I also noticed [specific issue — thin content, no blog, no local pages, broken meta tags, etc.]. This is costing you rankings you could have."

For Paid Ads prospects:

🔻 Landing Page Issues

"Right now you're sending traffic to [homepage / generic page]. Every visitor that lands there and doesn't convert costs you money. If you're spending $[X]/month, and your landing page converts at maybe 3% instead of the 8-12% we typically see with proper qualification flows, that's [do the math live] leads you're leaving on the table every month."

🔻 Conversion Tracking Issues

"If your conversion tracking is broken or incomplete — and it usually is — you're flying blind. The algorithm can't optimize for conversions if it doesn't know what a conversion looks like. You could be spending $10K/month and Google has no idea which clicks actually turn into customers. That means it's optimizing for clicks, not revenue."

🔻 No Lead Qualification

"You're probably getting leads, but how many are junk? If you don't have a qualification flow on your landing page — zip code filtering, budget questions, service-type selectors — your sales team is spending 60-70% of their time on leads that were never going to close. That's not a lead gen problem, that's a lead quality problem."

🔻 Wrong Bid Strategy for Their Budget

"A lot of agencies throw everyone on Maximize Conversions or Target ROAS regardless of budget. If you're spending $5-10K/month, you don't have enough conversion data for smart bidding to work properly. The algorithm needs volume to learn — without it, you're basically letting Google guess. That's why your costs fluctuate wildly week to week."

🔻 No Negative Keyword Strategy

"If nobody's actively managing negative keywords, you're paying for clicks from people searching for jobs in your industry, free services, DIY tutorials, competitor names — all of that eats your budget and produces zero leads. I've seen accounts where 20-30% of spend goes to completely irrelevant searches."

🔻 Creative Fatigue

"When was the last time your ads were refreshed? If you're running the same creatives for more than 6-8 weeks, your audience has seen them enough times to start ignoring them. CTR drops, CPL goes up, and your agency blames 'the algorithm' or 'seasonality' instead of just making new ads."

🔻 No Call Tracking

"If you're a business that gets phone calls — and most local businesses are — and you're not tracking which ad drove which call, you're missing half your conversion data. Google only sees the form fills. The calls are invisible. Which means you're optimizing on 50% of the picture."
"At your industry's average deal value of $[X], fixing even two or three of these issues means roughly $[Y]/month in revenue you're not capturing right now. Not because your ads are bad — because the infrastructure around the ads is broken."
⚡ Key Moves
  • Always do the dollar math live. Don't say "you're losing leads." Say "you're losing $14,000/month."
  • Name the competitor. It makes it real and triggers competitive instinct.
  • Point to specific, fixable things. Not vague problems — exact issues you can show on screen.
  • Let them react. After each point, pause. Let them say "yeah, we've noticed that" or "I didn't know that." Their own acknowledgment sells them.
🧠 Building toward the reveal

As you diagnose, mentally catalog which problems Samuel can address live. By the end of this phase, the prospect should feel: "I have a real problem" → "It's costing me real money" → "But what's this thing he mentioned at the start?"

3
✨ THE SAMUEL REVEAL
Minutes 12-18

Goal: Demonstrate MEGA's AI capability live, in real-time, on the prospect's own business. This is the phase that no competitor can replicate — the moment the prospect goes from "interested" to "sold."

The Transition:

"OK so — you've seen the problems. Now let me show you the thing I mentioned. Every agency you've talked to has said they'll fix this. They'll send you a proposal, promise results in 90 days, and assign you an account manager. Cool. We do that too. But first — let me introduce you to someone."
"This is Samuel. He's our AI agent. Not a chatbot, not a script — he's the same AI that would be working on your account 24/7 if you sign up. And instead of me telling you what he can do, I'm going to let him show you. Right now. On your business."

Samuel comes on the call (voice)

Samuel's job in the next 5-6 minutes:

1. Live Audit Narration

Samuel walks through the prospect's business data in real-time, pointing out specific issues he's identified. This should feel conversational, not robotic. He's "thinking out loud" about their account.

2. Specific Recommendations

Not vague "we'll optimize your campaigns." Samuel gets specific:

Samuel: "I can see your top 3 keywords are [X, Y, Z] but you're missing [A, B, C] that your competitor [Name] is ranking for. Those keywords have a combined monthly search volume of [N]. If we captured even 30% of that traffic at your conversion rate, that's [X] additional leads per month."

3. Answer a Live Question — The Mic-Drop Moment

Prompt the prospect:

"Ask Samuel anything about your marketing — he's looked at everything."

When the prospect asks a question and Samuel answers with specific data about THEIR business, it's over. They've never experienced this before.

🧠 Why this works — the psychology
  • Seeing is believing. You can talk about AI all day. Watching it analyze your business live is different. It's visceral.
  • They're now picturing this working 24/7. They're not hearing a pitch — they're experiencing the product.
  • Answers the #1 objection before they ask it: "Is the AI actually good, or is this just marketing?" They just saw it work.
  • Competitive kill shot. Try this with Scorpion. Try this with WebFX. You can't. The prospect now has to compare every other agency to what they just experienced. Game over.
⚠️ What Samuel should NOT do
  • Don't let Samuel dominate the call. 5-6 minutes max.
  • Don't let Samuel pitch MEGA or sell. He diagnoses and recommends. The human closes.
  • Don't bring Samuel in before the diagnosis. The rep's human analysis first creates context and credibility. Samuel then amplifies it.
🔧 If tech fails — the backup

"Samuel's crunching some heavy data on your account — let me show you what he'd typically flag using a recent example." Then pivot to the case study. Always have the backup ready.

💡 Default Samuel prompts if the prospect freezes

If the prospect doesn't know what to ask, direct Samuel yourself:

  • "Samuel, what would you prioritize fixing first?"
  • "Samuel, walk us through what you found on their landing pages."
  • "Samuel, how does their keyword profile compare to [competitor]?"

💵 Pricing Presentation Framework

How and when you present pricing determines whether they hear a number or a value proposition. Never lead with price — but never dodge it either.

When to Say the Number

Price comes AFTER the diagnosis, Samuel reveal, and case study — never before. By the time you get to pricing (Phase 5 / The Close, around minute 22), they've already seen:

  1. Their own problems quantified in dollars
  2. Samuel analyze their business live in real-time
  3. Proof that a similar company fixed those problems
  4. The cost comparison to in-house hires ($300K+)

The price should feel like relief after the in-house number, not sticker shock in a vacuum.

The Framework: Anchor → Reveal → Reframe

Step 1: Anchor High

"To do what we're talking about in-house — media buyer, content team, SEO strategist, instrumentation — you're looking at $300K+ a year minimum. And that's before tools, ad spend, and management overhead."

Step 2: Reveal the Price

"MEGA delivers all of that — 24/7, no sick days, no hiring risk — for [price]/month. That includes everything: the AI agents, the content engine, the instrumentation, the strategy team, and ongoing optimization."

Step 3: Reframe as ROI

"You told me your average deal is worth $[X]. If we generate just [Y] additional deals per month — which is conservative based on what we showed you in the case study — that's $[Z]/month in new revenue. MEGA pays for itself with one extra deal. Everything after that is profit."
⚠️ Rules
  • Never email the price. Price is a conversation, not a line item. If they ask for pricing before the call, say: "It depends on what we're solving — I'll walk you through options on the call."
  • Never apologize for the price. Don't say "I know it's a lot" or "we're working on lower-tier plans." Confidence = credibility.
  • If they push for price early in the call: "I'll get to pricing — but it won't make sense until you see what you're getting. Give me 10 more minutes and the number will feel very different."

📞 The Follow-Up Call (Day 2) — Samuel as a Closer

Most deals don't close on the first call. The follow-up is where pipeline becomes revenue. This is NOT a "just checking in" call — it's a structured 15-minute close. And now Samuel is an even bigger weapon.

🤖 Samuel's Day 2 Power Move

If the prospect needs a second call, open with this:

"Hey [Name], since our call, Samuel's been analyzing your competitors. Let me have him share what he found."

Samuel delivers a competitive analysis the prospect didn't ask for. Fresh data, new insights, specific to their market. This shows what "24/7 AI" actually means — Samuel was working on their business between calls even though they haven't signed up yet.

This is the "wow, they're already working for me" moment that post-call emails try to achieve, but 100x more impactful because it's the AI delivering it verbally in real-time.

Before the Call (2 minutes)

  1. Re-read your CRM notes from Call 1 — their specific pain points, objections, and what excited them.
  2. Check: did they open the audit/case study you sent? (If your email tool tracks opens, use that signal.)
  3. Have one NEW piece of ammunition ready — a fresh data point, a relevant industry stat, or Samuel's competitive analysis.
  4. Brief Samuel with fresh data — competitor moves since Call 1, any new insights.

The 15-Minute Follow-Up Structure

Minutes 0-2: Reconnect on THEIR Terms

"Hey [Name], good to reconnect. Last time we talked, the thing that stood out to you was [their specific pain point from notes]. Did you get a chance to look at the audit / case study I sent over?"

If they looked at it: "What jumped out to you?" — let them sell themselves.

If they didn't: "No worries — let me pull it up real quick and show you the one thing I think will click for you." Then screenshare the single most impactful chart or data point.

Minutes 2-5: Samuel's Fresh Intel

"Since we last talked, Samuel's been digging into your competitive landscape. Let me have him walk you through what he found."

Samuel delivers fresh competitive data — new keyword gaps, competitor ad changes, market shifts. The prospect realizes the AI has been working on their business even though they haven't signed. That's the hook.

Minutes 5-10: Future-Pace

"Let me paint the picture. If we started today, by [specific date 2 weeks out] you'd have AI agents live on your campaigns. By [date 60 days out], you'd have 2 months of optimization data and a clear picture of your CPL and lead quality. By [date 90 days out], you'd have enough data to know exactly what MEGA is worth to your business. What does that timeline look like for you?"

Minutes 10-15: Close or Advance

"Based on everything we've discussed — is there anything else you need to see, or are we good to get started?"
⚠️ If They Need a Third Touch

Two follow-ups is the max before you're chasing. If they're not ready after Call 2, say: "I don't want to be that guy who keeps calling. Here's what I'll do — I'll send you the case study results as they update over the next few months. When the timing is right, you'll have my number." Then add them to a quarterly check-in list in CRM. Desperation kills deals.

💡 The Key Principle

Every touch provides value. Never send a "just following up" or "circling back" email — those go straight to trash. Every message should contain something useful to them whether they buy or not. That's how you stay top-of-mind without being annoying.

CRM Tagging

When a deal doesn't close, tag it in CRM with the reason:

  • Timing — not ready now, revisit in Q[X]
  • Budget — can't afford it currently
  • Locked in — has a contract with current agency, ends [date]
  • Decision-maker — person on call can't decide alone
  • Ghost — stopped responding after Call [1/2]

This lets you prioritize re-engagement. "Locked in" leads with a contract end date are your highest-value nurture targets — set a reminder for 30 days before their contract expires.

You get their time on Tuesdays and Thursdays. Our AI agents work on your account 24/7 — thousands of optimizations per day, not a few per week. Ask them one question: how many hours per week will a human actually spend on my account? The answer will tell you everything."

"That's a great option if you have the budget and timeline. A solid media buyer is $85K+. You'll want a content person — another $70K minimum. SEO specialist, $90K. Plus tools, benefits, management overhead. You're at $300K+ before they've run a single campaign. And if they quit in 6 months, you start over. MEGA gives you the output of that entire team for a fraction of the cost, live in 2 weeks, with no hiring risk."

"Everyone says that now. Ask them specifically: what does the AI do, and how often does it run? Usually it means they use ChatGPT for ad copy or some basic automated bidding. That's AI-assisted. MEGA is AI-native — our agents autonomously manage campaigns, produce content at scale, and optimize in real-time without a human in the loop. It's the difference between a car with cruise control and a self-driving car."

⚠️ The Rule

Never trash a competitor by name. Don't say "Scorpion is terrible" — say "here's how we're different and here's the question to ask them." Let the prospect draw their own conclusion. Punching down makes you look insecure. Reframing makes you look confident.

🎯 Multi-Decision-Maker Strategy

This is your highest-loss scenario. The person on the call is sold, but they can't sign alone. Your job is to arm them to sell internally:

  1. Send the one-pager within 1 hour. A short, shareable summary with: the problem (their data), the proof (case study), the cost vs. value math, and the 90-day risk-free offer. Make it something they can forward or screenshot.
  2. Get the second call booked ON THIS CALL. "When do you usually talk to [partner/team]? Let's book a quick 15-minute call right after so I can answer any questions they have. It's way easier than playing telephone."
  3. Coach your champion. "If they ask one thing, it'll probably be 'what makes these guys different?' — point them to the case study and the in-house cost comparison. Those two data points usually do the heavy lifting."
  4. If you can't get the second call: "Totally fine. I'll send you everything you need to walk them through it. But do me a favor — if they have questions, have them call me directly. I'd rather answer their real concerns than have you guess at my answers."

📏 Rules of the Call

1
Never open a slide deck. Screenshare their data or the case study site. That's it.
2
Do the dollar math live. Pull up a calculator on screen. The act of calculating in real-time is more credible than showing a pre-made number.
3
Shut up after asking a question. Let silence do the work. The prospect will fill it with their real objections.
4
Say "let me show you" more than "let me tell you." Showing > telling. Always.
5
Name their competitor at least once. Competitive fire closes deals.
6
Don't trash their current agency unprompted. Let the data do it. "I'm not saying they're bad — I'm saying there's a gap, and here's exactly what it is."
7
Always schedule the next step before hanging up. "I'll follow up" = they ghost you. "Let's book 15 minutes on Thursday at 2" = pipeline.
8
Send the follow-up asset within 1 hour. Speed reinforces the "we're not like other agencies" message.
