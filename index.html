The Philosophy

Traditional agency sales calls are 30 minutes of talking about yourself. This call is 30 minutes of talking about them — their problems, their wasted money, their missed opportunities.

You're not pitching. You're diagnosing. By the time you present MEGA, they've already convinced themselves they need to change.

Pre-Call Prep (5-10 min — NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Before every call, you need ammunition. This is what separates MEGA from every agency pitch they've sat through.

🤖 Automated Pre-Call Briefs

We're building an automated system that generates everything you need before each call — SEO gaps, competitor data, keyword counts, ad waste signals, industry benchmarks — all in one brief, delivered to you before the call starts. When you're running back-to-back sales calls all day, you don't have 10 minutes to manually research each prospect. The system does it for you so you walk into every call armed and ready.

What the brief covers (and what to review before each call):

  1. Quick SEO snapshot — Their domain's current keyword count, traffic estimate, top 3 position keywords, content gaps vs. competitors.
  2. Competitor comparison — Their biggest local/industry competitor with side-by-side keyword counts.
  3. Industry case study loaded — The relevant case study from the case study site, ready to screenshare.
  4. Their current situation — Website, landing pages, any visible ad activity. Anything broken (no tracking pixel, homepage as landing page, no qualification flow, etc.).
  5. Dollar math ready — Their industry's average deal value, typical CPL ranges, and close rates so you can do live math on the call.
💡 If they gave ad account access

Review it. Find 2-3 obvious problems you can point to live. This alone closes deals.

The Call Flow

1
DISARM
Minutes 0-3

Goal: Break the "agency pitch" pattern. Make them feel like this is different.

Open with:

"Hey [Name], thanks for taking the time. Before we get into anything — I want to be upfront. I'm not going to walk you through a slide deck or tell you how great we are. I actually did some homework on your business already, and I'd rather spend this time showing you what I found. Sound good?"
✅ Why this works
  • Every other agency opened with "tell me about your business" or a logo slide. You're immediately different.
  • You've signaled competence before saying a single thing about MEGA.
  • "I did homework" triggers curiosity — they want to see what you found.

Then qualify quickly (3 questions):

"Before I share what I found — quick context:"

1. "Are you currently investing in SEO or Paid Ads today?"
2. "Are you doing it with an agency or in-house?"
3. (If paid ads) "What's your monthly ad spend?"

That's it. Three questions — tells you everything you need to tailor the rest of the call. Don't interrogate. You'll learn everything else by showing them their own data.

2
THE DIAGNOSIS
Minutes 3-12

Goal: Create urgency by showing them problems they didn't know they had. This is the "holy shit" phase.

🔍 Use Ray Feng's Audits

Ray built custom auto-generated audits that pull all of this data automatically — SEO gaps, competitor keyword counts, content gaps, landing page issues, tracking problems. Pull up the audit on the call and walk them through their own data. The audit IS the diagnosis. It does the heavy lifting so you can focus on the conversation.

Screenshare the audit. Walk them through their data.

For SEO prospects:

"So I pulled your domain. You're currently ranking for [X] keywords. Your top competitor, [competitor], has [Y] keywords. That gap represents traffic — and revenue — that's going to them instead of you."
"You have [X] pages on your site. [Competitor] has [Y]. In SEO, content volume builds topical authority — Google sees them as the expert in your space because they've covered more ground. Right now, you're bringing a knife to a gunfight."
"I also noticed [specific issue — thin content, no blog, no local pages, broken meta tags, etc.]. This is costing you rankings you could have."

For Paid Ads prospects:

🔻 Landing Page Issues

"Right now you're sending traffic to [homepage / generic page]. Every visitor that lands there and doesn't convert costs you money. If you're spending $[X]/month, and your landing page converts at maybe 3% instead of the 8-12% we typically see with proper qualification flows, that's [do the math live] leads you're leaving on the table every month."

🔻 Conversion Tracking Issues

"If your conversion tracking is broken or incomplete — and it usually is — you're flying blind. The algorithm can't optimize for conversions if it doesn't know what a conversion looks like. You could be spending $10K/month and Google has no idea which clicks actually turn into customers. That means it's optimizing for clicks, not revenue."

🔻 No Lead Qualification

"You're probably getting leads, but how many are junk? If you don't have a qualification flow on your landing page — zip code filtering, budget questions, service-type selectors — your sales team is spending 60-70% of their time on leads that were never going to close. That's not a lead gen problem, that's a lead quality problem."

🔻 Wrong Bid Strategy for Their Budget

"A lot of agencies throw everyone on Maximize Conversions or Target ROAS regardless of budget. If you're spending $5-10K/month, you don't have enough conversion data for smart bidding to work properly. The algorithm needs volume to learn — without it, you're basically letting Google guess. That's why your costs fluctuate wildly week to week."

🔻 No Negative Keyword Strategy

"If nobody's actively managing negative keywords, you're paying for clicks from people searching for jobs in your industry, free services, DIY tutorials, competitor names — all of that eats your budget and produces zero leads. I've seen accounts where 20-30% of spend goes to completely irrelevant searches."

🔻 Creative Fatigue

"When was the last time your ads were refreshed? If you're running the same creatives for more than 6-8 weeks, your audience has seen them enough times to start ignoring them. CTR drops, CPL goes up, and your agency blames 'the algorithm' or 'seasonality' instead of just making new ads."

🔻 No Call Tracking

"If you're a business that gets phone calls — and most local businesses are — and you're not tracking which ad drove which call, you're missing half your conversion data. Google only sees the form fills. The calls are invisible. Which means you're optimizing on 50% of the picture."
"At your industry's average deal value of $[X], fixing even two or three of these issues means roughly $[Y]/month in revenue you're not capturing right now. Not because your ads are bad — because the infrastructure around the ads is broken."
💡 If you have ad account access
"I found [specific waste — broad match bleeding, no negative keywords, wrong bid strategy, broken conversion tracking]. This alone is costing you roughly $[X]/month. That's not an optimization problem — that's a plumbing problem."
⚡ Key Moves
  • Always do the dollar math live. Don't say "you're losing leads." Say "you're losing $14,000/month."
  • Name the competitor. It makes it real and triggers competitive instinct.
  • Point to specific, fixable things. Not vague problems — exact issues you can show on screen.
  • Let them react. After each point, pause. Let them say "yeah, we've noticed that" or "I didn't know that." Their own acknowledgment sells them.
3
THE BRIDGE
Minutes 12-15

Goal: Transition from "here's your problem" to "here's proof it's fixable" — WITHOUT pitching yet.

"So the gap is clear. The question is — how fast can you close it, and what does it actually take? Let me show you what happened with a [their industry] company in a similar situation."

Walk through the TRAJECTORY, not just the result:

"This is a [industry] company that came to us [X] months ago. They were at [starting metric]. Here's month 1 — [metric]. Month 3 — [metric]. Month 6 — [metric]. Month 8 — [metric]."
"What I want you to notice isn't the end number. It's the curve. Month 1 is setup. Month 2 you start seeing movement. By month 3-4, the compounding kicks in. This isn't luck — it's a system producing predictable improvement every single month."

For SEO — real proof points:

"HubiFi came to us ranking for 117 keywords. 16 months later — 20,000 keywords. They went from 29 visitors a month to 30,000. We published 320 articles that built topical authority so deep that Google basically had no choice but to rank them."
"Bluestone Law — 274 keywords to 4,000 in 5 months. 30 visitors to 2,500. A law firm. In 5 months."

For Paid Ads — walk the monthly data:

"Month 1, their cost per qualified lead was $447. By month 3, $271. By month 8, $203. Every month the AI agents learned more about which leads convert, which audiences waste money, which hours of the day perform. That's thousands of optimizations no human team could make manually."
4
THE REFRAME
Minutes 15-20

Goal: Position MEGA as a fundamentally different category. Not agency vs. agency — AI workforce vs. human hire.

"Let me be honest about something. We're not an agency. I know that sounds like a line, but hear me out."
"A traditional agency assigns you a media buyer who manages 15-20 accounts. They give yours maybe 2 hours a week. They check in on Thursdays. If a keyword starts bleeding $200/day on Monday, they catch it Thursday — that's $800 gone."
"What we deploy is an AI workforce. Agents that monitor your campaigns 24/7, make bid adjustments thousands of times per day, catch waste in hours instead of days. You're not getting a person who juggles your account with 19 others. You're getting a system that treats your account like it's the only one."

The cost comparison:

"A senior media buyer costs $85K+ a year and manages your one account. A content team to produce what Lindsay produces would cost you $120K+ minimum. An SEO strategist, another $90K. You're looking at $300K+ to build in-house what we deliver for a fraction of that. And our agents don't take PTO, don't get pulled into meetings, and don't have bad weeks."

Dollar math for their specific situation:

"You told me you're spending $[X]/month on ads. If we improve your CPL by even 25% — which is conservative based on what we've done in [their industry] — that's [math] more leads per month at the same spend. At your average deal value, that's $[X] in additional revenue. The ROI isn't theoretical — it's arithmetic."
5
THE TIMELINE
Minutes 20-23

Goal: Make speed a selling point while being honest. Turn the realistic timeline into a competitive advantage.

"Here's exactly what the first two weeks look like — and I'm going to be more specific than any agency has ever been with you:"
📅 The 14-Day Launch Sequence

Days 1-3: Sign up, complete onboarding, give us access to what we need — ad accounts, analytics, CRM. Simple stuff.

Days 4-5: Our instrumentation team gets to work. Tracking pixels, tag manager config, landing pages with qualification flows, CRM integration. Simultaneously, you meet your paid ads strategist for strategy, goals, and first round of creatives.

Day 6: SEO kickoff with your account manager. Content strategy mapped, keyword targets locked.

Days 6-14: Everything gets wired up and tested. By day 14, we're live and the AI agents are deployed on your account.

"Most agencies take 4-6 weeks to 'onboard' you — which is code for 'we're busy with other clients.' We go live in two weeks because our agents do the heavy lifting, not overworked account managers."
6
THE CLOSE
Minutes 23-28

Goal: Remove risk, create urgency, get the commitment.

The Confidence Play:

"Here's what I'll tell you. Give us 90 days. If the content quality, the keyword trajectory, the lead quality — if any of it doesn't impress you, walk away. We're that confident because we've seen this system work across [X] industries now."
"The only risk is waiting. Every month you don't fix [the specific problems you showed them], that's another $[X] in revenue going to your competitors. That's not a scare tactic — you saw the numbers yourself."

Handle "I need to think about it":

"Totally understand. What specifically do you want to think through? Is it the budget, the timing, or something else?"

If budget:

"What are you spending now on [current agency/in-house]? And what results are you getting? Because the question isn't whether you can afford to try something new — it's whether you can afford to keep paying for what's not working."

If trust / burned before:

"I get it. You've been burned. That's exactly why I showed you month-by-month data instead of promises. And that's why I'm saying 90 days with a clear exit — because I don't need to lock you in. The results will keep you."

The Final Push:

"Tell you what — I can get your onboarding started today and have your strategist scheduled by [day]. By [two weeks out], you'll have AI agents live on your account doing work your current setup physically cannot do. What would you need to see to feel good about moving forward today?"
7
NEXT STEPS
Minutes 28-30

Goal: If they say yes — lock it in immediately. If they need a follow-up — make it count.

If they're ready:

"Great. Let me send you the payment link right now so we can lock this in. Once you complete payment, you'll be redirected straight into onboarding — takes about 30 minutes to fill out. That's how we get access to everything we need to hit the ground running. I'm also going to introduce you to an onboarding specialist right away who'll help you through it and make sure we're set up to go live as fast as possible."
⚠️ Critical

Send the intro email with the onboarding specialist right away. The moment they pay, fire off the intro so there's zero gap between payment and getting started. Don't let momentum die.

If they need a follow-up:

"No problem. I'm going to send you something after this call — not a generic deck, but a custom breakdown specific to your business. Take a look, and let's reconnect [specific day/time]. I'll also send over the [industry] case study so you can dig into the data yourself."
⚠️ Critical

Schedule the follow-up ON the call. "I'll follow up next week" = dead lead. "Let's book 15 minutes on Thursday at 2" = pipeline.

Post-Call (Within 1 Hour)

  1. Send the personalized asset — NOT "nice to meet you, here's our deck." Send a custom audit/report for their business generated by Ultron or Ray's tool. This is the "holy shit, they already did work for me" moment.
  2. Send the relevant case study link — The specific industry one from the case study site.
  3. If follow-up scheduled: Add to CRM with notes on their specific pain points and objections. Your follow-up call should reference exactly what they said.

🛡️ Objection Responses

"We've been burned by agencies before."
"So have most of our clients. That's actually why they switched. The difference is I just showed you their data — month by month, not a cherry-picked testimonial. And I'm offering 90 days to prove it. If your last agency was willing to do that, you'd probably still be with them."
"We're happy with our current agency."
"That's great. What are your current keyword rankings / CPL / ROAS numbers? [They usually don't know, which proves the point]. Here's what I'd suggest — let us run an audit, totally free, no strings. If your current agency is killing it, the audit will confirm that and we'll shake hands. If there are gaps... you'll want to know about them either way."
"Your pricing is too high."
"Compared to what? A full-time media buyer is $85K. A content team is $120K+. An SEO specialist is $90K. We're replacing all three for less than one. The question isn't 'is MEGA expensive' — it's 'what's the alternative costing you in results?'"
"I need to talk to my partner/team."
"Absolutely. Would it help if I put together a quick summary with the numbers we discussed? That way you can walk them through exactly what we found and what the opportunity looks like. And would it make sense for them to join a quick 15-minute follow-up? I can walk them through the same audit."
"Can you guarantee results?"
"I can guarantee the system — 24/7 AI optimization, more content than any agency can produce, real-time adjustments. The results depend on your market and offer, but here's what I can show you: [pull up case study]. Every single client in this industry saw improvement by month 2. I'm not asking you to take my word for it — the data's right there."
"We tried AI tools before and they didn't work."
"Which ones? [Listen]. Those are tools — they need a human to operate them. MEGA is an AI workforce. Lindsay doesn't need someone to tell her what to write. The ad agents don't need someone to log in and apply recommendations. They run autonomously, 24/7. The difference between an AI tool and an AI agent is the difference between giving someone a hammer and hiring a construction crew."

📏 Rules of the Call

1
Never open a slide deck. Screenshare their data or the case study site. That's it.
2
Do the dollar math live. Pull up a calculator on screen. The act of calculating in real-time is more credible than showing a pre-made number.
3
Shut up after asking a question. Let silence do the work. The prospect will fill it with their real objections.
4
Say "let me show you" more than "let me tell you." Showing > telling. Always.
5
Name their competitor at least once. Competitive fire closes deals.
6
Don't trash their current agency unprompted. Let the data do it. "I'm not saying they're bad — I'm saying there's a gap, and here's exactly what it is."
7
Always schedule the next step before hanging up. "I'll follow up" = they ghost you. "Let's book 15 minutes on Thursday at 2" = pipeline.
8
Send the follow-up asset within 1 hour. Speed reinforces the "we're not like other agencies" message.
